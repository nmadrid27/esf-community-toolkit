# THINK Level, Project 2: Visual Essay

**Assigned:** Week 3
**Due:** Week 6, Friday, final panels + process documentation

---

## Brief

Create a visual essay of 3 to 5 panels that communicates a personal idea, experience, or argument through image and (optionally) text. The essay must have a point of view: it is not a collection of related images, but a sequence that builds toward something.

You choose the medium, the subject, and the visual approach. What matters is that the essay says something you mean, and that the viewer can follow your reasoning through the visual sequence.

---

## Requirements

**The essay must:**
- Contain 3 to 5 panels with a coherent visual relationship
- Communicate a clear point of view (argument, reflection, narrative, or question)
- Demonstrate intentional sequencing: the order of panels matters and is justified

**Your documentation must include:**
- Position Statement (written before significant AI engagement)
- At least 1 Record of Resistance documenting a deliberate choice about AI output
- AI Use Log with entries for each session where AI was used
- A short process reflection (250 to 500 words)

---

## Evaluation

Projects will be assessed on:
- Point of view: does the essay have something to say, and is it yours?
- Visual coherence: do the panels work together as a sequence?
- Craft: does the execution serve the concept?
- Process integrity: does your documentation show your thinking, not just your result?

---

## Notes

Tool selection is yours. You are not required to use any specific software, medium, or AI tool. AI may be used as a research tool, a brainstorming partner, or a compositional reference. AI-generated images may serve as reference material but may not appear as final artwork without substantial transformation.

Your Position Statement is due before you begin working with AI on this project. Write it first. It anchors everything that follows.
