# Epistemic Stewardship Framework: Quick Reference

## The Problem

AI tools can produce polished output faster than you can think through the problem. The risk is not that AI produces bad work. The risk is that you accept good-sounding work you cannot defend, did not direct, and cannot honestly claim as yours.

## The Method

Five practices, applied in sequence:

### 1. Position Statement

Before AI enters any project, write down:
- **Your stance:** What is your position, argument, concept, or approach?
- **What matters most:** What is the core that must survive in the final work?
- **Non-negotiable:** What will you not compromise on?

This does not need to be polished. Bullet points and rough notes are fine. What matters is that it captures your direction before AI can shape it.

### 2. Five Questions

At every decision point (accepting AI output, submitting a draft, publishing), ask:

| Question | What it checks |
|----------|----------------|
| Can I defend this? | You understand every claim and choice well enough to justify it |
| Is this mine? | You directed the work, not just accepted AI's framing |
| Did I verify? | You checked facts, sources, logic, or behavior independently |
| Would I teach this? | You understand it well enough to explain it to someone else |
| Is my disclosure honest? | Your attribution accurately describes both contributions |

If any answer is no, stop and fix it.

### 3. Records of Resistance

When you reject, revise, or override an AI suggestion, document:
- What AI suggested
- What you did instead
- Why (referenced to your Position Statement)

Records of Resistance are evidence of judgment. They prove you evaluated AI output against your own intent and made a deliberate choice.

### 4. AI Use Log

For each project, track:
- Tool and version used
- What you asked the AI to do
- What it produced
- What you kept, changed, or rejected
- What you contributed independently

### 5. Disclosure

Attach an honest statement to finished work describing:
- What AI contributed
- What you contributed
- How you verified the result

Match the detail level to the stakes: a quick internal note needs less than a published article or client deliverable.

## The Workflow

```
POSITION → EXPLORE → BUILD → VALIDATE → DISCLOSE
```

1. **Position:** Write your Position Statement. No AI.
2. **Explore:** Bring AI in as a thinking partner. It challenges your position.
3. **Build:** Work with AI. Log contributions. Record resistance.
4. **Validate:** Run the Five Questions. Fix failures.
5. **Disclose:** Write honest attribution.
