# Position Statement: Visual Essay on Identity and Place

**Jamie Torres, BFA Illustration | THINK level**
**Project 2: Visual Essay**

---

## Position

I want to make a visual essay about the places that shaped me before I had language for them. Three places: my grandmother's kitchen in Laredo, the public library on Corpus Christi Bay where I spent summers, and the bus stop bench on San Bernardo where I waited every morning for two years. These are not important places. Nobody would photograph them for a magazine. But they are where I learned to see.

The essay should feel like memory, not documentation. Fragmented. Some parts sharp, some parts soft. I do not want a linear narrative. I want the viewer to move through the panels the way I move through these memories: out of order, with some details impossibly vivid and others fading at the edges.

## Emphasis

Specificity over sentiment. These are real places with real details: the mint green walls, the water stain on the library ceiling shaped like a boot, the crack in the bench where ants came through in April. The details carry the feeling. I do not need to add feeling on top of them.

I want to avoid nostalgia as a visual style. No sepia. No soft focus across everything. No generic "warmth." If a panel is warm, it is because the light in that room was warm, not because I am performing nostalgia.

## Non-negotiables

- Every panel must include at least one observed detail that I remember specifically, not a detail I am inventing or borrowing from reference images
- The color in each panel comes from my memory of the actual place, verified against family photos where possible, not from an AI's idea of what the place "should" look like
- Final illustrations are hand-rendered. AI can help with composition and research, but the marks on the page are mine
