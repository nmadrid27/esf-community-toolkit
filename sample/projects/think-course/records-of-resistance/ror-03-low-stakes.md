# Record of Resistance: 03

**Project:** Place and Light Photo Series | **THINK level | Sam Chen, BFA Photography**
**Date:** Week 6, Tuesday

---

## What the AI Suggested

I asked the AI to review my artist statement draft for clarity. It suggested changing "These photographs sit with the light rather than capture it" to "These photographs embrace the natural light of each location to create an authentic sense of place."

---

## What I Chose Instead

I kept my original sentence. I did accept two other edits from the same review: a comma splice fix and a paragraph reorder that improved the flow.

---

## Why

"Embrace the natural light" and "authentic sense of place" are exactly the kind of generic artist statement language I hear in every critique. They sound professional but say nothing. My sentence is stranger, and that is the point. "Sit with" is deliberate. It implies patience, stillness, duration. The photographs are long exposures. The camera sits with the light the way a person sits with another person. That verb carries the concept of the project.

The AI smoothed my language into something more conventional. Convention was not what I needed.

---

## What Happened

My professor circled "sit with the light" during the statement review and wrote "yes, this is your voice" next to it. The phrasing came up again during critique; two classmates said it changed how they looked at the exposures.

---

## Would I Change This Decision?

No. This was a small edit, one sentence. But it is a good reminder that the AI's instinct is to normalize language, and normalized language is the enemy of a strong artist statement. Documenting it took thirty seconds. The habit matters more than the scale.
