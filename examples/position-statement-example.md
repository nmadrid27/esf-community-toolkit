# Position Statement

**Project:** Personal Portfolio Redesign
**Date:** 2026-03-20 (Written before any AI use on this project.)

---

## Element 1: My Stance

*What creative direction or question am I exploring? What is my point of view going into this project?*

> I am rebuilding my portfolio from scratch. The current site uses a template I downloaded two years ago and it does not represent my work anymore. My practice has shifted toward systems design and environmental graphics, and the portfolio should reflect that: structured, spatial, and confident. I want visitors to understand my point of view as a designer before they see a single project image.

---

## Element 2: What Matters Most

*What qualities or values are non-negotiable in this project? What must be preserved regardless of what AI suggests?*

> The visual identity has to be mine. Not a clean minimal template that could belong to any designer. The typography, the grid structure, the palette: all of these come from my existing visual language. My current work uses a lot of negative space, heavy type, and a restricted palette of black, off-white, and one accent color (a deep terracotta I have used consistently since 2024). That palette stays. The site should feel like an extension of the work, not a container for it.

---

## Element 3: What I Will Not Compromise

*The line AI will not cross. What decisions, judgments, or creative choices remain entirely mine?*

> AI does not choose my typography. AI does not choose my color palette. AI does not decide how projects are sequenced or what gets featured. Those are editorial decisions that reflect my values and my read on the work. AI can help me with layout structure, copy editing, and code generation, but the decisions about what to say and what to show are mine.

---

## After the AI Session

*Return here after working with AI. Did your stance hold? Did it shift? Name the change.*

**Drift level:** minor
**What shifted:**

> I ended up adding a short "About" section that the Companion suggested when it noticed the site had no context for who I am. I had not planned to include one. After thinking about it, I agreed it was missing. I wrote the copy myself.

**Was the shift your decision, or did you follow AI's framing without questioning it?**

> My decision. I sat with the suggestion for a few minutes before agreeing. The reasoning was sound: visitors had no way to understand my practice without it.
