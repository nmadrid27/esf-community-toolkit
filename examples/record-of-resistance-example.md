# Record of Resistance

**Project:** Personal Portfolio Redesign
**Date:** 2026-03-22
**Record #:** 1

---

## What AI Suggested

*Describe the specific AI output you chose not to follow. Be concrete: what did it produce, recommend, or generate?*

> The Companion generated a five-color palette for the portfolio site: soft sage green, warm cream, dusty rose, slate gray, and charcoal. It described the palette as "approachable, editorial, and contemporary." It also suggested this palette would work well with a light-weight sans-serif typeface like Inter or Neue Haas Grotesk to create "a refined, modern portfolio aesthetic."

---

## Why I Rejected or Revised It

*What was wrong with the suggestion? Did it conflict with your Position Statement, miss context, flatten your intent, or feel like someone else's work? Name the reason.*

> The palette is generic. Sage-and-cream is everywhere right now: every design portfolio I have seen in the past six months uses some version of this combination. More importantly, it directly conflicts with my Position Statement. I wrote that my palette stays: black, off-white, and deep terracotta. That palette is not an aesthetic preference; it is part of my visual identity. I have used it consistently across projects since 2024. Switching to something "contemporary" would make the portfolio look like someone else's work.
>
> The typeface suggestion had the same problem. The Companion defaulted to safe, widely-used options. My brand uses Freight Display for headings, which is editorial and slightly idiosyncratic. Replacing it with Inter would flatten the identity.

---

## What I Did Instead

*What decision did you make? What did you create, write, or design in place of the AI's suggestion? How is your version different?*

> I kept my existing palette: black (#0A0A0A), off-white (#F5F2ED), and terracotta (#C4593A). I kept Freight Display for headings and switched to Freight Text for body copy to create a consistent typographic system. The palette and type system are now documented in a `design-tokens.md` file so the Companion can reference them in future sessions and will not suggest alternatives.
