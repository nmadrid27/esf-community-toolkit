# Record of Resistance

**Project:** Dissertation Chapter 3 — Literature Review
**Date:** 2026-02-28
**Record #:** 2
**Discipline:** Academic research (educational technology)

---

## What AI Suggested

I asked the Companion to help me organize the theoretical frameworks section. It grouped the literature into three clusters: constructivist learning theory, sociocultural approaches, and ecological systems theory. The clustering was clean and the framing was confident. It wrote a transitional paragraph arguing that the three clusters form a "convergent theoretical foundation" for my study.

---

## Why I Rejected or Revised It

The clustering missed the tension I am trying to argue in this chapter. Vygotsky and Bronfenbrenner are not converging toward the same thing — they have genuinely different units of analysis (the dyad versus the nested system), and that difference matters for how I frame my methodology in Chapter 4. The AI collapsed a productive theoretical tension into a synthesis I do not actually believe.

The phrase "convergent theoretical foundation" is the kind of confident academic filler that reviewers flag. It asserts agreement without demonstrating it. My committee will ask me to defend that claim, and I cannot.

---

## What I Did Instead

I kept the three-cluster structure because it is genuinely useful for orienting the reader. But I rewrote the transition to make the tension explicit: "These three frameworks share a commitment to situated, socially embedded cognition, but they diverge in how they model the unit of development — a divergence this study must account for rather than resolve." Chapter 4 methodology section will explain how I navigate that divergence in my study design.

The revision is longer and less tidy than the AI's version. It is also more honest about what my argument actually is.
