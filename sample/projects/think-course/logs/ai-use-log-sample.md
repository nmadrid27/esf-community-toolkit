**Jamie Torres, BFA Illustration | THINK level, Project 2**

## Session 1: Composition Research

**Date:** Week 4, Tuesday
**Duration:** 45 minutes
**AI Tool Used:** AI chat assistant
**Phase:** Explore

**What I asked the AI to do:**
Help me think through compositional approaches for the grandmother's kitchen panel. I described the space (small galley kitchen, mint green walls, window on the far end, doorway on the near end) and asked for suggestions on how to make the viewer feel inside the room rather than looking at it from outside.

**What the AI produced:**
Three compositional options: (1) a vanishing-point view through the doorway, (2) a fish-eye distortion to show more of the narrow space, (3) a cropped close-up that implies the room through objects.

**What I kept / revised / rejected:**
- Kept option 1 (vanishing point through doorway). It solved the spatial problem I was stuck on.
- Rejected option 2. Fish-eye feels gimmicky for what I am trying to do.
- Filed option 3 as a possibility for a different panel.

**The AI also recommended a warm palette (burnt sienna, ochre, terra cotta). I rejected it entirely. See RoR-01.**

**Verification:** N/A for composition suggestions. Palette rejection based on my own observation and family photos.

**Five Questions check:** Q2 flagged. The vanishing point was the AI's idea, not mine. I tested it against my position statement ("feel like memory, not documentation") and decided it serves the concept. I can defend the choice on its own terms.

## Session 2: Artist Statement Draft Review

**Date:** Week 5, Thursday
**Duration:** 20 minutes
**AI Tool Used:** AI chat assistant
**Phase:** Make

**What I asked the AI to do:**
Review my artist statement draft for clarity and grammar. I pasted the full 300-word draft and asked for a readability pass: fix errors, flag unclear sentences, do not add ideas.

**What the AI produced:**
Edited version with 6 suggested changes: 2 grammar fixes (comma splice, subject-verb agreement), 1 paragraph reorder, and 3 rephrased sentences.

**What I kept / revised / rejected:**
- Kept the 2 grammar fixes. Straightforward corrections.
- Kept the paragraph reorder. Moving the "specificity over sentiment" paragraph earlier made the statement stronger.
- Rejected all 3 rephrased sentences. They smoothed out language that was intentionally rough. My voice matters more than polish here.

**Verification:** Grammar fixes checked against my own knowledge. Reorder evaluated by re-reading the full statement in both orders.

**Five Questions check:** All yes. The content is entirely mine. The AI improved clarity without changing meaning (except for the three sentences I caught and rejected).

## Session 3: Reference Image Research

**Date:** Week 6, Monday
**Duration:** 30 minutes
**AI Tool Used:** AI chat assistant
**Phase:** Make

**What I asked the AI to do:**
Help me find examples of visual essays and illustrated memoirs that use fragmented panel layouts, specifically artists working with memory and place. I wanted reference for how other illustrators handle the "some parts sharp, some parts fading" quality I described in my position statement.

**What the AI produced:**
A list of 8 artists and works, with brief descriptions of their approach to fragmented narrative.

**What I kept / revised / rejected:**
- Kept 3 references I could verify: looked up the artists, found their work, confirmed the descriptions were accurate.
- Rejected 2 references I could not verify. The AI described specific works that did not appear in any search I ran. Possible hallucinations.
- Set aside 3 others for later investigation when I have library access.

**Verification:** Searched each artist name and work title independently. Confirmed 3, flagged 2 as unverifiable, deferred 3.

**Five Questions check:** Q3 (Did I verify?) was the critical question here. Two of the AI's references did not hold up. Good reminder to check everything, even when the descriptions sound specific and detailed.
